Elizabeth J. Fienler

Elizabeth Feinler was an American computer programmer who was instrumental in the development of the Internet. She played a key role in creating the first online directory of resources, called the ARPANET Directory Service, which served as the basis for the modern Domain Name System (DNS). Her work helped to revolutionize the way we access information and connect with each other online.

A Mini-Biography

Born in 1931 in the United States, Feinler started her career in computer science at the Stanford Research Institute (SRI) in 1960. There she led the development of the Network Information Center (NIC) and the first version of the ARPANET directory. In 1983, she oversaw the creation of the DNS and the transition to the current system of domain names. Feinler was also involved in the creation of the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF), an organization that helps to develop and maintain Internet standards.

Feinler received numerous awards and honors for her contributions to the development of the Internet, including the IEEE Internet Award in 2000 and the Internet Society's Postel Award in 2012. She passed away in 2021 at the age of 89, leaving behind a legacy of groundbreaking work that has shaped the modern Internet.
