Rosalind Franklin

Rosalind Franklin was a British chemist and X-ray crystallographer who made significant contributions to the study of molecular structures, particularly in the field of DNA. Her work with X-ray crystallography played a critical role in the discovery of the structure of DNA, which was essential in understanding the genetic code and the mechanisms of inheritance.

A Mini-Biography

Born in 1920 in London, Franklin obtained her Ph.D. from the University of Cambridge and went on to work at King's College in London. It was there that she conducted the research that led to the famous "Photo 51" image of DNA, which was key in the discovery of the double helix structure of DNA. Unfortunately, Franklin passed away at the age of 37 before receiving recognition for her crucial contributions to the field.

Despite her untimely death, Rosalind Franklin's work continues to be celebrated and recognized today. Her legacy has inspired countless scientists, particularly women, to pursue careers in STEM fields and has paved the way for further breakthroughs in the study of molecular biology.
