katherine Johnson

Katherine Johnson was an American mathematician and NASA employee who made significant contributions to the United States' space program. Her work included calculating the trajectories for the first human spaceflight and the Apollo 11 mission to the moon. She was also one of the first African-American women to work as a NASA scientist.

A Mini-Biography

Born in 1918 in West Virginia, Johnson showed an early aptitude for mathematics and graduated from high school at the age of 14. She later attended West Virginia State College and eventually began working for NASA's predecessor, the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA), in 1953. She faced discrimination and challenges as a woman and a person of color in a male-dominated and predominantly white field, but she persevered and became a highly respected and valued member of the NACA and NASA teams.

Throughout her career, Johnson was recognized for her exceptional mathematical skills and was awarded numerous honors, including the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2015. She passed away in 2020 at the age of 101, leaving behind a legacy of groundbreaking work and trailblazing accomplishments.
